There is a certain kind of small, soft music a tassel makes when it brushes against your wrist, your hipbone, the inside of your ankle as you walk. It is barely a sound at all — more a feeling than a noise — the gentlest *swish-swish* of silk floss meeting silk floss, the faintest sway of a knotted bead at the end of a long, fringed strand. And yet, once you have worn a tassel for an entire spring afternoon, you begin to understand why women across the Pink City of Jaipur have been knotting these tiny wind-chimes for centuries. A tassel is not a decoration. A tassel is a heartbeat made of thread.

This season, the [Soul Flow Apparel](https://soulflowshop.com) story is being quietly tied up in those very heartbeats.
