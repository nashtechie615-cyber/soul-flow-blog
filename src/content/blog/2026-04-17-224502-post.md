## Why butter yellow feels different

Most yellows demand attention. They shout. They compete. Butter yellow does the opposite — it *softens* everything around it. It catches light the way cream catches candle-glow, diffusing rather than reflecting, hugging the skin instead of overpowering it. Stylists at Vogue and The Zoe Report have been calling it "the new neutral," and honestly, they're right. It plays like beige, but with a secret heartbeat. It whispers joy without ever raising its voice.

Paired with crisp white, it becomes a Provence daydream. Pressed against [wide-leg cotton in clean ivory](https://soulflowshop.com/products/white-wide-leg-beach-cotton-pants), it turns into the kind of outfit that makes strangers in cafés ask where you got everything. Layered with soft boho texture — lace, eyelet, ruffles, hand-crochet — it becomes unmistakably *you*.
