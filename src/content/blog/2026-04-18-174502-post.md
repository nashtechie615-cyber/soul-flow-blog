There is a print returning to spring this year that feels less like a trend and more like a memory. It arrives on the breeze of a slow Sunday, curls itself around a vintage scarf tied to a wrist, and whispers against the hemline of a tea-length dress drifting through a flea market. Paisley — those swirling, teardrop-shaped motifs with their origins in ancient Persia and their heart forever wedded to the free-spirited '70s — is having the softest, most sun-soaked revival, and Spring 2026 is wearing it beautifully.

What makes paisley feel so right for this particular season is its refusal to be anything other than itself. It does not apologize for taking up space. Those inked droplets, those curling vines and botanical flourishes, they move across fabric the way incense smoke moves through a quiet room — slow, deliberate, and hypnotic. Whether it's printed on a gauzy kimono, a silk headscarf, or the softest cotton blouse, paisley carries a kind of storytelling woven right into its shape. Every motif is a tiny universe. Every repeat feels like a verse of a poem you half-remember.

This spring, designers are leaning into paisley's richest, most romantic side. Think jewel-toned backgrounds — garnet, indigo, forest, saffron — layered with creamy teardrops and golden thread accents. Think the kind of prints that look hand-painted, faded just slightly at the edges, as if they'd already lived one beautiful life before finding you. It pairs effortlessly with the rest of the boho wardrobe: [flowing silhouettes](https://soulflowshop.com), denim cut-offs softened by a thousand washes, strappy leather sandals, and stacks of thin silver bangles that catch the afternoon light.
